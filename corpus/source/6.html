“Michelin is missing out”
Reviewed June 28, 2013

As a seasoned traveler and sybarite, which my age supports, I was astounded that LE Restaurant, Amsterdam, did not have Michelin Stars. It is comparable to Saüc in Barcelona or Agli Tre Amici in Udine, Italy. Superb ensemble of selections very assembled by a professional and affectionate team. The wine list was adequate, but needs to be expanded a bit more exclusive to meet the menu's nuances. Do not even think of showing up without a reservation!

“everything was perfect”
Reviewed May 31, 2013

I ve made a reservation a few weeks earlier for I knew it is not possible to get a table in this restaurant any other way. My husband and I were celebrating our 20iest year together so for starters I think we had the best table. A large 2 persons table overlooking the restaurant and with a nice view of the small (very quiet!)kitchen. Delicious amuses to start, good suggestions for wine and all courses came in a really good pace so not to quick not to slow. Because there was a lot to taste I cannot remember everything but I can recall: a beautifully made petit gamba tosti, macaron of mackerel, lovely soft cod, the most delicious beef I ve eaten, a little cheese after and for me a bit too much sweets ; ) at the end...(dessert plus extra freshly baked madeleine cakes, and more sweets served alongside the coffee. But heh, I don t have a sweettooth. Very lovely place for intimate dining. Not for familys with young children or large groups.

“Vaut le détour, really!”
Reviewed April 16, 2013

All superlatives used by previous customers are completely justified. There is one menu with small modifications (don't do it) if you insist. The amuses are very refined, predicting a marvellous diner. I'm living 40 years in Amsterdam and I can assure you: this is one of the best culinary experiences of our city. I try to go at Le Restaurant about 4 times a year, each time it is the same magic. The price of this marvellous experience: in februari 2013 we paid 72.50€ for the menu, excl the wines, coffee etc.
There is one problem: many local gourmets know the place, so reservation is a must, not 1-2 but 15-30 days before your planned visit, especially on Friday and Saturday. So, if your are visiting Amsterdam and do not want to be disappointed, make your reservation before leaving your country.

“Sublime fun”
Reviewed April 15, 2013

Le Restaurant is a top class restaurant , with a friendly , intimate style that is always full of fun. Set in the ground floor front rooms of a house in De Pip & so away from the central tourist area, it has only 24 covers or so . The menu offers a seemingly endless array of amuses, fish, meat , cheese and desert dishes - all exquisite, and all a little different . What makes Le restaurant special though are the people - all , the waiter, sommelier and chef , want you to have a good time , and take the time to talk to you if you want.
Having been with 3 times , both business and personal , in the last year , I can confirm that you never have a bad experience.
